The credential behind this

I planned this trek the same way you're planning yours — YouTube, Reddit, ChatGPT, every travel blog I could find. I spent weeks on research. And I still couldn't answer the questions that actually mattered before I left.

"Which teahouse on Day 3? What does altitude sickness actually feel like at 3,700m alone? Is Deurali to MBC manageable when the weather turns bad?"

I went anyway. Day 4 was the hardest — visibility down to 10 metres, no other trekkers on the trail, a solo call whether to push to Machhapuchhre Base Camp or descend. I pushed through. I reached ABC at 4,130m.

That experience is the reason you can trust this planning. Not because I'm a certified guide — because I figured out every variable on that trail without one, and I can now make sure you don't have to.
